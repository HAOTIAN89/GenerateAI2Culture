ANYTHING TO IMAGE 
Learning Cultures

A project to showcase how AI understands our culture and where are its limits to differentiate what’s ours.

START GENERATION

AI meeting Humanities

A project that puts AI to test in understanding cultures of the world in 4 basic dimensions, food, clothing, music and art, showcasing how it can understand us as humans and our ways.

Project team

Objective

The Objective of the project is to prove how far can an AI model understand the cultural differences among 2 cultures presented, we will try to run search/generation processes to prove its understanding.

The DataBase

The databases created were not used to train the model but to prove if it can understand the cultures around the world once provided a multimedia database of them.

Features

The model used can receive either image, text or audio as an input for the two functions that it supports which are search for images or generation of images. The inputs can be isolated or combined for generation.

The model

The model we used is the combination of ImageBind unified latent space and Stable-Diffusion-2-1-unclip. It should be the most powerful multimedia search and generation open-source model which can run on the normal computers nowadays.

Features of the model and the project

Explain search and generation models as well as how inputs can create new images

Search

The model we used is the combination of ImageBind unified latent space and Stable-Diffusion-2-1-unclip, we expect for the model to reach a performance close to the situation where  our search model can distinguish the different culture staffs (such as Renaissance Oil paintings and Rococo Oil paintings, or piano and organ).

Generation

The ability of the models to recognize, differentiate, and accurately generate traditional and culturally specific items. This involves understanding the nuances of clothing styles, patterns, and significance across different cultures, recognition of famous artworks, and also the correct depiction of dishes and an understanding of its cultural significance. 

Input

Data set generated manually with more than 300 samples analyzed between different cultures over time and also thta occupy a different greographical space.
